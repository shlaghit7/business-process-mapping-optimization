# Business Process Mapping & Optimization Case Study

## Project Overview

This project demonstrates how Business Process Analysis and Process Optimization techniques can be used to identify operational inefficiencies, reduce delays, improve productivity, and lower business costs.

The case study focuses on an **Order-to-Cash (O2C)** business process where workflow activities were analyzed, bottlenecks were identified, and optimization opportunities were proposed using business analysis methodologies and data-driven decision-making.

The project simulates a real-world consulting engagement commonly performed by Business Analysts, Process Analysts, Operations Teams, and Management Consultants.

---

## Business Problem

Organizations often face:

* Long process cycle times
* Manual approval delays
* High operational costs
* SLA violations
* Process bottlenecks
* Customer complaints
* Resource inefficiencies

The objective of this project is to analyze the current process, identify inefficiencies, and recommend improvements to enhance operational performance.

---

## Project Objectives

* Map the existing business process (As-Is)
* Identify bottlenecks and delay points
* Analyze process performance KPIs
* Perform root cause and gap analysis
* Design an optimized future-state process (To-Be)
* Recommend automation opportunities
* Build an interactive Power BI dashboard
* Estimate business impact and cost savings

## Key Insights

### Major Bottlenecks

* Manager Approval
* Payment Collection
* Invoice Processing

### Operational Findings

* 68% of delays originated from approval and payment activities.
* Finance department showed the highest error and rework rates.
* Invoice creation generated significant customer complaints.
* Manual workflows contributed heavily to SLA breaches.

### Optimization Opportunities

* Workflow Automation
* Automated Invoice Generation
* Online Payment Integration
* Real-Time Inventory Visibility
* Approval Workflow Optimization
